# How Redundant Are Redundant Color Adjectives? An Efficiency-Based Analysis of Color Overspecification

## Metadata

- Authors: Paula Rubio-Fernández
- Year: 2016
- Venue: Frontiers in Psychology, 7:153
- DOI / URL: 10.3389/fpsyg.2016.00153
- Local PDF path: `10-writing/literatures/Rubio-Fernández - 2016 - How Redundant Are Redundant Color Adjectives An E.pdf`
- Tags: overspecification, color adjectives, redundancy, efficiency, referential communication, English, Spanish, adjective position

## One-Paragraph Summary

This paper argues that redundant color adjectives (RCAs) are not violations of the Gricean Maxim of Quantity but can be *efficient* because they exploit color contrast in the visual display to facilitate listener object identification. Two production experiments show that RCA use is driven by (1) the efficiency of color in the visual context (more RCAs in polychrome than monochrome displays; more in English pre-nominal than Spanish post-nominal position) and (2) the semantic category of the noun (more RCAs for clothes than geometric shapes; more for atypical than stereotypical colors). The paper introduces the key distinction between *informativeness* (whether an adjective is necessary for unique reference) and *efficiency* (whether it facilitates visual search), proposing the latter as the better explanatory framework.

## Research Question

- What factors drive the production of redundant color adjectives, and can they be explained without treating overspecification as a violation of cooperative principles?

## Method / Data

- Design: Two language production experiments (object requests), within-subjects
- Experiment 1: English and Spanish speakers; polychrome vs. monochrome displays; clothes vs. geometric figures
- Experiment 2: English speakers; atypical vs. variable vs. stereotypical colors
- Measures: Rate of redundant color adjective production
- Analysis approach: Mixed-effects logistic regression; comparison across display type, language, and noun category

## Key Findings

1. Speakers produce more RCAs in polychrome displays (where color is a distinctive visual cue) than monochrome displays.
2. English speakers produce more RCAs than Spanish speakers, attributed to the greater efficiency of pre-nominal modification for visual search.
3. RCAs are more frequent for clothes than geometric figures, and for atypical colors than stereotypical colors — reflecting how central color is to the object category.

## Core Takeaways For The Current Project

- Establishes the foundational distinction between **informational value** (contrast among competitors of same kind) and **discriminatory value** (color contrast across entire display). This distinction is central to understanding why our RSA speaker model might prefer color-first ordering.
- Pre-nominal position (English) yields more RCAs than post-nominal (Spanish): a key cross-linguistic prediction that motivates the incremental efficiency hypothesis developed in later papers.
- The efficiency framing reframes "redundancy" as a rational, cooperative behavior — directly relevant to RSA pragmatic reasoning.

## Limitations / Boundary Conditions

- Production-only study; no comprehension or RT data confirming that RCAs actually speed up visual search (addressed in 2021 paper).
- Comparison of English and Spanish conflates adjective position with other cross-linguistic factors.
- No formal model; efficiency is argued qualitatively.

## Useful Quotes (Short)

- "An alternative analysis is proposed, which focuses on the efficiency and pertinence of color in a given referential situation." (abstract)
- "Since overspecification happens precisely in contexts where the encoded property is not necessary to establish unique reference, speakers' choice of a longer referential expression needs to be explained." (p. 2)

## Relevance To Current Writing Tasks

- Supports: RSA speaker model producing color-modified descriptions when discriminatory value is high, even without unique reference requirement
- Contrasts with: Standard Gricean / RSA accounts that treat overspecification purely as informativeness violations
- Open questions for us: Does our RSA model reproduce the polychrome > monochrome pattern? Does the global vs. incremental speaker differ in their RCA rates?
