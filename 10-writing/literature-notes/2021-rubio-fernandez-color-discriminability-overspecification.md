## One-Paragraph Summary

This paper provides both theoretical and empirical support for the *visual efficiency hypothesis*: redundant color words can be more efficient than shorter descriptions when they facilitate the listener's visual search via color discriminability. A psychophysical framework is developed that integrates pragmatics, visual perception, and social cognition. Eye-tracking evidence (Exp. 1) confirms that listeners find the referent faster when a redundant color adjective is used and color is distinctive (pop-out effect). A production experiment (Exp. 2) shows that speakers systematically reduce their use of redundant color adjectives as the target color becomes more pervasive in the display. The paper also introduces the Incremental Collaborative Efficiency (ICE) model which predicts these patterns quantitatively.

## Research Question

- Can redundant color words be more efficient than shorter descriptions? Is speakers' use of overspecification sensitive to a target's discriminability in the visual context?

## Method / Data

- Design: Two experiments — (1) eye-tracking comprehension (listener finds referent faster with vs. without redundant color word) and (2) language production (speaker use of redundant color adjectives as function of target color prevalence)
- Measures: (1) fixation/response times; (2) rate of redundant color adjective production across display types
- Analysis approach: Mixed-effects models; comparison across discriminability conditions

## Key Findings

1. Listeners are faster to identify "the blue star" than "the star" when color is distinctive (pop-out display), confirming that redundant color words have positive discriminatory value for visual search.
2. Speakers systematically reduce redundant color adjective use as color prevalence increases in the display — showing sensitivity to the target's discriminability.
3. The ICE model reproduces known qualitative patterns of overspecification (density effect, monochromaticity effect, prenominal > postnominal) without additional fitted parameters.

## Core Takeaways For The Current Project

- First direct RT evidence that redundant color adjectives actually speed up visual search — confirms the efficiency framing empirically, not just theoretically.
- Introduces **informational value** (preempts ambiguity among same-kind competitors) vs. **discriminatory value** (facilitates search across entire display): the distinction central to our model's speaker utility function.
- The ICE model is a direct computational antecedent of our incremental RSA speaker — understanding what it does and does not model is important for situating our contribution.

## Limitations / Boundary Conditions

- The pop-out displays are relatively artificial; discriminability effects may be subtler in naturalistic scenes.
- ICE model predicts qualitative patterns but has not been fit to slider preference data of the kind we collect.
- Theory of Mind / social cognition component is argued qualitatively but not formally modeled.
